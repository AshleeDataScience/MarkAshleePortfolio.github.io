Exploratory Data Analysis

This personal project is rooted in my experience at my first company, Taiyo Yuden Philippines. Initially, I joined as a product engineer and later transitioned to the Tech department to work as a data analyst. This shift is a common trajectory for engineers, particularly those in electronics and communications, within the company. In this role, I collaborated with data scientists and data engineers to conduct exploratory data analysis on non-conforming products. Our primary objective was to design dashboards for real-time monitoring of product history, sourcing data from machines through IoT technology.

During the exploratory data analysis phase, our focus was on identifying the root cause or machine(s) responsible for a significant portion of non-conforming products in inductors. Utilizing available data from our database, I mapped out specific machines, conducted sampling, performed Fault Tree Analysis (FTA), and traced issues to their origins. Ultimately, our investigation led us to a specific component within a particular machine. Upon replacing this component, we observed a significant improvement in product quality.

Lot Number Segregation

In this phase, our aim is to identify the lot numbers affected and pinpoint the primary issues associated with specific inductors. The data collected for this analysis is sourced from the company's database. From our findings, it is evident that the predominant lot number affected is "3R3M A," with the primary issue identified as "Core Chipping."

Winding or Coating Machine?

Next, we need to ascertain whether the cause of the chipping originates from the coating machines or the winding machines. It is apparent that Winding Machine 375 is responsible for producing a significant number of core chipping ""END"" products as evident on the Winding Machine 75 corechipping trend slide.

Before or after winding?

Additionally, I conducted a Fault Tree Analysis to establish whether core chipping occurs before or after the winding process. Before delving into this analysis, I initially investigated whether core chipping might be attributed to other parameters, such as dimensions, or if it was merely a misinterpretation by the technicians. However, based on the data, it is highly probable that the core chipping issue is indeed originating from the winding machine itself, as we can see, the result of the chipping is really due to winding process.

Which chuck number has an issue?

During this phase of data analysis, we presented the Japanese executives with a detailed explanation of our sampling methodology, demonstrating how we systematically assessed each chuck to identify any damage. Through this process, we pinpointed Chuck No. 6 as the primary contributor to the core chipping damages. Furthermore, visual evidence supports our findings, as it is apparent from images that the chuck holder associated with Chuck No. 6 is indeed broken.

Rsult after chuck replacement

Following the chuck replacement, we replaced the broken chuck with a new one. Subsequently, on the final slide, we observed a significant decrease in core chipping issues with Winding Machine 75 in the November data. This reduction in non-conforming products has positively impacted the business, driving increased profitability.
